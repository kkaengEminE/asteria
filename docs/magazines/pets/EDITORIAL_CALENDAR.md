# Pets Magazine Editorial Calendar

## Purpose

This calendar defines the first 100 articles for the Pets Magazine. It is an operational publishing plan: editors may adjust dates in response to seasonality, reader feedback, search demand, or safety updates, but should preserve the priority logic and internal-link structure.

## Planning Rules

- **P1:** Foundational reader need, strong search intent, or important safety topic. Publish early and review regularly.
- **P2:** Valuable supporting coverage that expands a P1 cluster or serves a defined audience segment.
- **P3:** Narrow, exploratory, or lower-urgency coverage. Publish after its supporting cluster exists.
- **Evergreen:** Relevant throughout the year. Review at least annually or when guidance changes.
- **Seasonal:** Publish or refresh four to six weeks before the relevant season or event.
- **Monetization:** Indicates an editorially appropriate opportunity, not a requirement. Every recommendation remains subject to need, safety, and disclosure review.
- **Internal links:** Article numbers are the primary candidates. Add links only where they genuinely help the reader take a next step.

The article number is the suggested publishing order. P1 foundations lead each content cluster; supporting P2 and P3 articles follow once their internal-link targets are available.

## Publishing Cadence

- Start with two articles per week until the review workflow is stable; increase volume only when every article continues to pass the Quality Checklist.
- Publish in numerical order by default. Move seasonal articles forward only when their lead time begins, then resume the next unpublished number.
- Publish at least one P1 article per week during the first 20 weeks.
- Do not publish a P2 or P3 article before a referenced P1 foundation unless the link can be added promptly after the foundation is live.
- Maintain a balanced monthly mix of behavior, daily care, health and safety, enrichment, and one commercial-intent article at most per four editorial articles.
- Review Founder scores and reader signals every ten articles. Adjust briefs and ordering, but retain rejected or postponed topics in the editorial record with a reason.

## Calendar Maintenance

- Assign an owner, brief date, draft date, review date, and target publication date when an idea enters production.
- Record the primary query, reader intent, required sources, safety reviewer, and monetization disclosure needs in the article brief.
- Recheck seasonal timing quarterly and health or safety guidance whenever authoritative recommendations change.
- Update internal links after each publication batch so that new articles connect back to established foundations and foundations point to useful supporting guides.
- Do not replace editorial judgment with traffic or affiliate projections. Accuracy, animal welfare, and reader trust remain the release criteria.
