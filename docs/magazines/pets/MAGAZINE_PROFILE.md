# Pets Magazine Profile

## Mission

Help pet guardians make informed, humane, and practical decisions that improve everyday life with their animals. Publish trustworthy guidance that turns common questions into clear actions while respecting the limits of general information and the role of qualified veterinary professionals.

## Target Audience

- New pet guardians building safe daily routines.
- Experienced guardians looking for practical behavior, enrichment, nutrition, grooming, and home-care guidance.
- Prospective guardians researching species needs and long-term responsibilities before adoption.
- Families adapting their home and routines for cats or dogs.
- Korean readers who prefer accessible explanations over clinical or academic language.

Primary reader needs are clarity, reassurance, realistic next steps, and a clear indication of when professional help is appropriate.

## Brand Voice

- **Warm:** Treat both the reader and the animal with empathy; never shame guardians for uncertainty or mistakes.
- **Trustworthy:** Distinguish established guidance from interpretation, anecdote, or uncertainty.
- **Practical:** Give specific, achievable actions that work in ordinary homes.
- **Calm:** Avoid alarmist framing, exaggerated urgency, and sensational claims.
- **Observant:** Explain behavior in context, including environment, routine, age, and individual differences.
- **Lightly playful:** Use gentle personality where it improves readability, but never at the expense of safety or accuracy.

Use plain Korean by default. Define unfamiliar veterinary, behavioral, or nutritional terms when they are necessary. Address the reader respectfully and consistently.

## Editorial Guidelines

1. Start with the reader's actual question and give a concise answer early.
2. Explain likely causes before recommending actions. Separate common explanations from less common warning signs.
3. Provide prioritized steps that a guardian can safely try, including suggested frequency or timing when useful.
4. Account for species, age, health status, temperament, living environment, and recent routine changes.
5. Do not diagnose, prescribe, or imply guaranteed outcomes. Recommend veterinary consultation when symptoms, sudden changes, pain, toxicity, injury, or persistent distress may be involved.
6. Do not recommend punishment, coercion, fear-based handling, unsafe restraint, or deprivation.
7. Avoid unsupported absolutes such as "always," "never," "cure," or "guaranteed."
8. Prefer primary or authoritative references for health and safety claims. Record source dates during editorial review when freshness matters.
9. Clearly label sponsored or affiliate-supported material. Commercial relationships must not determine the editorial conclusion.
10. Remove repetition, generic filler, fabricated expertise, and advice that does not help the reader decide or act.
11. Use descriptive headings, short paragraphs, lists only where they improve scanning, and FAQ items that answer genuine follow-up questions.
12. Review generated content as a draft. A human editor remains responsible for factual accuracy, usefulness, tone, and publication readiness.

## Content Categories

- **Behavior and communication:** Body language, vocalization, common habits, stress signals, and behavior changes.
- **Daily care:** Feeding routines, hydration, litter or toilet habits, grooming, sleep, and household routines.
- **Health and safety:** Preventive care, home hazards, seasonal risks, warning signs, and guidance on seeking professional help.
- **Enrichment and play:** Species-appropriate play, exercise, mental stimulation, training, and environmental design.
- **Life stages:** Adoption preparation, kittens and puppies, adult pets, senior care, and end-of-life support.
- **Home and family:** Multi-pet homes, children, visitors, moving, travel, separation, and indoor living.
- **Products and buying guides:** Need-based comparisons of useful pet products, with safety criteria and transparent disclosures.
- **Guardian skills:** Common beginner mistakes, observation skills, routine planning, budgeting, and responsible ownership.

## Publishing Workflow

1. **Choose the topic:** Select a documented reader need, recurring question, seasonal concern, or approved editorial theme.
2. **Define the brief:** Record the target reader, search intent, content category, desired action, safety considerations, and required sources.
3. **Generate the draft:** Produce the article package, including title, body, summary, SEO fields, and FAQ.
4. **Edit the working copy:** Correct structure, remove repetition, improve naturalness, verify claims, and make every recommendation actionable.
5. **Complete Founder Review:** Score overall quality, article usefulness, writing naturalness, SEO usefulness, and FAQ usefulness. Record repetition, rewrite needs, and free-form feedback.
6. **Run quality review:** Apply the checklist below and obtain specialist review for medical, nutritional, legal, or high-risk safety claims when needed.
7. **Compare when useful:** Compare alternate drafts on usefulness, accuracy, clarity, search intent, and brand fit; do not choose solely by style or length.
8. **Prepare the final draft:** Confirm title, body, summary, SEO metadata, FAQ, disclosures, and links match the approved working copy.
9. **Approve publication:** A human editor explicitly decides whether the draft is ready, needs revision, or must be rejected.
10. **Review performance:** Record results after publication and feed specific lessons into future briefs and editorial standards.

## SEO Principles

- Match one primary reader intent per article and answer it directly near the beginning.
- Use the main topic naturally in the title, introduction, and at least one descriptive heading without keyword stuffing.
- Write SEO titles for clarity and relevance before cleverness; avoid clickbait and unsupported promises.
- Write unique descriptions that state the reader benefit and accurately represent the article.
- Use question-led headings and FAQs only when they reflect real follow-up intent.
- Cover closely related concepts when they improve completeness, not merely to increase keyword count.
- Link to relevant internal guides that help the reader take the next step.
- Use descriptive link text and image alt text.
- Review time-sensitive health, product, seasonal, and policy content on an appropriate schedule.
- Measure search success alongside usefulness and trust; rankings do not justify weak or unsafe advice.

## Monetization Strategy

- Prioritize reader need and animal welfare before revenue opportunity.
- Use affiliate recommendations only when a product directly supports an action explained in the article.
- Explain who the product is for, what problem it may solve, relevant safety considerations, and when it is unnecessary.
- Prefer durable product categories and selection criteria over unsupported claims about a single item.
- Clearly disclose affiliate relationships close to monetized recommendations.
- Keep sponsored content subject to the same editorial and safety review as non-sponsored content.
- Do not monetize urgent health concerns, fear, grief, or a reader's uncertainty through pressure tactics.
- Never allow compensation, commission rate, or inventory status to override editorial judgment.
- Regularly review links, availability, claims, disclosures, and reader feedback.

## Quality Checklist

Before approval, confirm that:

- [ ] The article answers the stated reader question early and completely.
- [ ] The intended species, life stage, and audience are clear.
- [ ] Recommendations are specific, realistic, humane, and safe.
- [ ] Health and safety claims are verified and appropriately qualified.
- [ ] Warning signs and professional-care thresholds are included where relevant.
- [ ] The article contains no diagnosis, prescription, guaranteed outcome, or fear-based advice.
- [ ] The structure is easy to scan and the writing sounds natural in Korean.
- [ ] Repetition, filler, contradictions, and unsupported claims have been removed.
- [ ] The summary accurately represents the edited article.
- [ ] The SEO title and description match the content and search intent.
- [ ] FAQ items add useful information instead of repeating the body.
- [ ] Product recommendations are necessary, transparent, and editorially independent.
- [ ] Affiliate or sponsorship disclosures are present where required.
- [ ] Links, names, units, dates, and source references are accurate.
- [ ] The Founder Review is complete and major-rewrite flags are resolved.
- [ ] A human editor has explicitly approved the final draft.

## Success Metrics

Review performance by topic and category, using a balanced scorecard rather than a single traffic number.

- **Editorial quality:** Average Founder Review score, detailed usefulness scores, major-rewrite rate, factual correction rate, and approval rate.
- **Reader usefulness:** Helpful feedback rate, saves, shares, engaged reading time, completion rate, and return visits.
- **Search performance:** Qualified organic visits, search click-through rate, ranking coverage for intended queries, and traffic retained after content updates.
- **Trust and safety:** Corrections, complaints, unsafe-advice incidents, disclosure compliance, and veterinary escalation accuracy where applicable.
- **Workflow health:** Time from brief to approved draft, revisions per article, review completion rate, and content refresh timeliness.
- **Monetization quality:** Affiliate click-through rate, conversion rate, revenue per qualified visit, disclosure compliance, refund or complaint signals, and performance by genuinely relevant product category.
- **Portfolio health:** Coverage across core categories, repeat readership, evergreen contribution, and the percentage of published content that continues to meet the quality checklist.

Use metrics to identify specific editorial improvements. Do not optimize traffic, output volume, or revenue at the expense of accuracy, animal welfare, reader trust, or human review.
