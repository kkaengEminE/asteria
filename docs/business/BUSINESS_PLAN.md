# Asteria Pets Magazine Business Plan

## Business Objective

Validate whether a Korean-language pet magazine can earn repeat readership and sustainable revenue by publishing practical, human-reviewed answers to real guardian questions. The first 180 days should prove audience demand, editorial reliability, organic acquisition, and ethical monetization before increasing publishing volume or building additional products.

## Target Audience

### Primary Audience

- Korean cat and dog guardians in their first two years of ownership.
- Prospective guardians researching costs, routines, safety, and long-term responsibilities before adoption.
- Guardians searching for practical explanations of everyday behavior, feeding, hydration, grooming, enrichment, and home management.

### Secondary Audience

- Experienced guardians adapting routines for senior animals, multi-pet homes, moving, travel, children, or changes in work schedules.
- Family members who share responsibility for a pet and need a clear, common reference.
- Readers comparing pet products after first understanding the need, safety criteria, and appropriate use.

### Core Reader Job

The reader should be able to understand what is probably happening, what can be safely checked or changed at home, and when professional veterinary or behavioral help is appropriate. Content should reduce uncertainty without diagnosing, exaggerating risk, or using emotional pressure.

## Publishing Frequency

### Days 1-30

- Publish three articles per week: two evergreen informational articles and one supporting or seasonal article.
- Launch with at least five human-approved articles so every first visitor can continue to a related guide.
- Review the Operation Log after every publishing batch.

### Days 31-90

- Maintain three articles per week until median human editing time and correction rate are stable.
- Add one commercially relevant buying guide for every four non-commercial articles at most.
- Refresh internal links every ten published articles.

### Days 91-180

- Increase to four articles per week only when the previous 20 articles completed human review, had no unresolved safety issue, and met the editing-time target.
- Keep at least 70% of output evergreen.
- Refresh seasonal content four to six weeks before its demand period and update high-traffic articles when guidance or products change.

Publishing volume is a capacity decision, not a success metric. Pause or reduce frequency when review quality declines.

## Traffic Acquisition Channels

### 1. Organic Search

Make Google and Naver search the primary acquisition channel. Build topic clusters around adoption, behavior, daily care, safety, enrichment, life stages, and home routines. Each article should satisfy one clear search intent and link to a relevant foundation article and next-step guide.

### 2. Naver Distribution

Adapt published articles into shorter Naver Blog or Post summaries that answer one question and link to the complete guide. Do not duplicate the entire article. Track referral visits and indexed brand searches separately.

### 3. Social Distribution

Turn practical checklists, warning signs, and routine examples into short Instagram or other visual posts. Use social channels to test reader questions and language, not as a substitute for the full source article.

### 4. Communities And Partnerships

Participate only where a published guide directly answers an active question. Avoid unsolicited link drops. Seek qualified links and citations from shelters, pet sitters, trainers, veterinary educators, and responsible adoption organizations after editorial trust is established.

### 5. RSS And Email Retention

Offer RSS from launch. Add a lightweight email digest after at least 20 articles are live and there is a reliable publishing rhythm. Send useful new or updated guides rather than promotional-only messages.

### Channel Measurement

Tag campaign links consistently and review acquisition by source, engaged sessions, return visits, and useful downstream actions. Do not judge a channel by raw clicks alone.

## SEO Strategy

### Search Intent

- Assign one primary query and one reader decision to every article brief.
- Prefer natural Korean questions and problem phrases over broad keywords or magazine-branded terms.
- Separate informational intent from product-comparison intent.

### Topic Clusters

- Publish a P1 foundation article before or alongside its P2 and P3 supporting articles.
- Link every new article to at least one established foundation when the link helps the reader.
- Update foundation articles with links to useful supporting coverage after each publishing batch.

### On-Page Standards

- Put the direct answer or decision frame near the beginning.
- Use one accurate H1, descriptive H2 sections, a unique SEO title, and a unique meta description.
- Use keywords naturally; never require a fixed density.
- Add FAQ items only for genuine follow-up intent that the body does not already answer.
- Use descriptive image filenames, captions when context is needed, and meaningful alt text.

### Trust And Freshness

- Cite current authoritative sources for medical, nutritional, toxicology, legal, or safety claims.
- Display publication and update dates.
- Identify the editorial owner and explain the human-review process.
- Review health and safety content when authoritative guidance changes and at least every 12 months.
- Correct material errors promptly and record the correction.

### Technical Search Readiness

Before launch, verify canonical URLs, HTTPS, mobile rendering, page titles, descriptions, sitemap, robots rules, structured crawlability, redirects, broken links, and Search Console ownership. Index only complete public pages.

## Monetization Roadmap

### Phase 1: Trust Before Revenue - Days 1-30

- Publish useful non-commercial coverage and establish disclosure, privacy, and editorial standards.
- Add affiliate links only to articles where a product is genuinely needed to complete an explained action.
- Record outbound affiliate clicks separately from editorial engagement.
- Do not place display advertising during the initial quality baseline.

### Phase 2: Affiliate Validation - Days 31-90

- Test affiliate recommendations in a small set of high-intent buying guides and relevant care articles.
- Explain selection criteria, limitations, safety considerations, and who does not need the product.
- Review broken links, availability, claims, disclosure placement, click-through rate, conversion, refunds, and complaints monthly.
- Remove recommendations that do not help readers, regardless of commission.

### Phase 3: Advertising Readiness - Days 91-180

- Consider display ads only after the site has stable organic traffic, sufficient content depth, required policy pages, and acceptable page performance.
- Start with restrained placements that do not interrupt the answer, obscure warnings, or imitate editorial recommendations.
- Compare revenue with changes in engagement, speed, and return visits before expanding placements.

### Phase 4: Digital Product Validation - Days 91-180

- Validate demand before production through reader surveys, waitlists, or preorder interest.
- Candidate products include adoption preparation worksheets, health-observation logs, household safety checklists, and routine planners.
- Build only when at least one recurring problem is supported by search behavior, reader feedback, and Operation Log evidence.
- Keep products educational and organizational; do not position them as veterinary diagnosis or treatment tools.

## Success Metrics

Metrics are targets for business validation, not guarantees. Review them with editorial quality, trust, and operational effort.

### 30 Days

- 12-15 human-approved articles published, including at least five P1 foundation articles.
- 100% of public articles indexed or submitted for indexing, with no unintended private or draft URL indexed.
- At least 10 non-brand search queries receiving impressions.
- At least 100 organic sessions and 25 returning users.
- Median Founder Review overall score of 4/5 or higher.
- Zero unresolved factual or animal-safety issues.
- Editing time measured for at least 80% of published articles.
- Baseline analytics, Search Console, and Operation Log data available for every publication week.

### 90 Days

- 36-45 human-approved articles published across at least five core categories.
- At least 3,000 monthly organic sessions.
- At least 25 non-brand queries in the top 30 and five in the top 10.
- Search click-through rate of at least 3% on pages with 500 or more impressions, reviewed by query intent.
- At least 10% return-visitor rate.
- Median human editing time at or below 30 minutes per article, without increased corrections.
- Major-rewrite rate below 20% over the latest 20 articles.
- Affiliate tests active on no more than 20% of articles, with disclosure compliance at 100%.
- At least five attributable affiliate conversions or enough click data to make a documented stop/continue decision.

### 180 Days

- 70-90 human-approved articles published, with at least 70% evergreen coverage.
- At least 15,000 monthly organic sessions.
- At least 100 non-brand queries in the top 30 and 20 in the top 10.
- At least 15% return-visitor rate or 500 combined RSS/email subscribers.
- Organic traffic distributed so that no single article contributes more than 30% of total organic sessions.
- Median Founder Review overall score remains at least 4/5.
- Material correction rate below 2%, with zero unresolved animal-safety incidents.
- Affiliate revenue covers recurring hosting and content-operation costs for one month, or a documented decision is made to change the model.
- Display-ad test completed only if traffic and user-experience requirements are met.
- At least 50 qualified waitlist or preorder signals for one digital product before production approval.

## Review Cadence

- Update `docs/operations/OPERATION_LOG.md` after every real editorial run.
- Review editorial and traffic data weekly for the first 30 days and monthly afterward.
- Make product improvements from repeated, measured operational evidence rather than isolated preferences.
- Revisit this plan at days 30, 90, and 180, recording which targets were met, missed, changed, or retired and why.
